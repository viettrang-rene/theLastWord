What do you like to do in your free time?

How to Answer

(1) Say what you like doing:

(2) Say why:

(1) I like to play football, (2) because it's fun and healthy.

Pronunciation "Liketa"

In natural speech, "like to" often sounds like "liketa":

  • I like to play football... → I liketa play football...
  • I like to go swimming... → I liketa go swimming...
  • I like to do yoga... → I liketa do yoga...

Expressions

Use these expressions to make your answer more interesting:

  • I'm quite into... (playing football)
  • I'm a big fan of... (playing football)
  • I'm keen on... (playing football)
  • I'm interested in... (learning new languages)
  • (the health benefits are) ...a huge plus

Grammar Notes

Like to vs. Like vs. Enjoy:

  • I like to play football / I like playing football / I enjoy playing football
  • I like to go swimming / I like (going) swimming / I enjoy (going) swimming
  • I like to do yoga / I like doing yoga / I enjoy doing yoga

Feelings:

  • Use -ing to talk about the thing causing the feeling: Skydiving is thrilling
  • Use -ed to talk about the person feeling the emotion: I was thrilled to hear...

Add Details

Make your answer more complete by adding details:

  • Who do you play with?
  • What do you do when you play football?
  • Where do you play?
  • When do you play?
  • Why do you play?
  • How often do you play?
  • How long have you been playing?

Example Answer

"In my free time, I enjoy playing football. I'm quite into outdoor activities and friendly competition, so I think football is a lot of fun. I also like to stay fit, so the health benefits of playing football are another huge plus. During the football season, I usually practice twice a week and play a match on the weekend. Most of the time, I play with my old school friends and some colleagues from work."

Adjectives for Activities

High-Energy & Exciting

  • Exciting - Playing video games with friends is always exciting.
  • Thrilling - Riding a roller coaster is a thrilling experience.
  • Entertaining - Watching a good movie is very entertaining.
  • Amusing - Listening to a comedy podcast can be quite amusing.
  • Invigorating - Going for a swim in the ocean is so invigorating.
  • Engaging - Debating a topic you're passionate about is really engaging.
  • Fascinating - Learning about ancient history is fascinating.

Calm & Fulfilling

  • Relaxing - Listening to quiet music is very relaxing.
  • Soothing - Gardening on a sunny afternoon is incredibly soothing.
  • Calming - Practicing yoga can be a calming way to start the day.
  • Rewarding - Volunteering for a good cause is a rewarding activity.
  • Fulfilling - Creating a piece of art is deeply fulfilling.
  • Satisfying - Finishing a challenging puzzle is so satisfying.
  • Inspiring - Visiting an art gallery can be very inspiring.
  • Refreshing - Taking a walk in the park is refreshing after a long day.

Word Differences

Fulfilling - Implies meeting a deep, long-term need for meaning and purpose by fully developing one's abilities, leading to overall happiness and growth.

Rewarding - Suggests that something offers immediate or tangible benefits, like knowledge, money, or the enjoyment of a particular experience, which may or may not lead to a deeper sense of purpose.

Satisfying - Simply means that a need or desire has been met, bringing contentment, pleasure, or gratification, which can be more immediate and less profound than fulfillment.
